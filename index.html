The Archive of Stars

A tribute the world never gave her… so I did.

Every film starts with something small — a click, a glance, a line.
Ours started with four words: ‘I miss you again.’
And the plot changed.

They say a true star doesn't enter the frame.
The frame bends to her.
The light follows her.
And you realize...
she’s the one we’ll remember.

Some scenes make time freeze.
Ours?
You, me, food in the car. Rain hammering the glass.
No script. Just feeling.

She never needed a line.
Her eyes do the acting.
Her silence is the dialogue.
And still, I hear her louder than every soundtrack I’ve known.

She didn’t save the world.
She saved me.
With small things —
like staying, listening, forgiving.

If the world watched her for one day —
they’d rise before the credits roll.
They’d cheer…
just because she existed.

Some stars fade.
But one…
waits quietly.
For June 5.
